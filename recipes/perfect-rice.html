Perfect Rice

To be a great homecook there are some basic dishes you have to absolutely master. Rice is one of those dishes. No need to buy a rice cooker, make this in a small pot with a lid. You can't go wrong with rice, pair it with anything! It also makes for great leftovers, overall a great go to dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 Cups White Rice
  • 2 1/2 Cups Chicken Broth
  • 2 TBSP Butter
  • 1 Diced Shallot
  • Kosher Salt and Pepper to tastb
  • Ground Thyme to taste
  • 1 Clove Smashed Garlic
  • 1 Bay Leaf

Preparation

  1. Dice up your shallot. Melt your butter slowly in a your pot as you dice.
  2. Toss your shallots into the pot, raise your heat to medium. Keep an eye on them as they cook, they will reach a translucent color when ready. About a minute or so.
  3. Toss your rice in the pot, mixing it with the shallots and allowing the butter to coat your whole rice. Allow it to toast for 30 seconds to a minute.
  4. Season your rice with salt and pepper as desired. Remember that your chicken broth and butter may also be salted and adjust your salt accordingly.
  5. Pour your chicken broth into the pot.
  6. Add your ground thyme to taste, a bay leaf, and your clove of smashed garlic. Stir your pot to mix in the spices, taste and adjust.
  7. By now your pot should be at or near a boil, if not don't worry, just wait it out. Once boiling, cover your pot of rice and bring the heat down all the way to low. Wait 15 minutes.
  8. At 15 minutes, your rice should be done or very near done, take a fork and taste. If you want a firmer rice, cook a minute or so less, if you want a softer rice, cook a minute or so more.
  9. Serve your rice and enjoy. Garnish with parsley or eat it as is, it's delicious either way.
